Japanese Curry

Japanese curry is a popular dish in Japan, often referred to as "curry rice". It is a mild and slightly sweet dish made with a roux-based sauce that is seasoned with a blend of spices, and typically contains meat, vegetables, and potatoes. The sauce is served over steamed rice, making it a hearty and filling meal. The dish has its roots in Indian cuisine and was introduced to Japan in the late 19th century, where it has since been adapted and modified to fit local tastes. Japanese curry is a beloved comfort food and is widely available at restaurants and cafes in Japan, as well as in instant form for home cooking.

Ingredients

  1. 1 large onion, diced
  2. 2 carrots, peeled and diced
  3. 2-3 potatoes, peeled and diced
  4. 1-2 lbs. of meat (chicken, beef, pork or a combination), cut into bite-sized pieces
  5. 2 tablespoons oil (vegetable, canola, or olive)
  6. 4 tablespoons curry roux (available in blocks or as a paste)
  7. 2-3 cups of water
  8. 1 teaspoon garam masala
  9. 1 teaspoon turmeric
  10. 1 teaspoon cumin
  11. 1 teaspoon coriander
  12. 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  13. Salt, to taste
  14. Steamed rice, for serving

Steps:

  1. In a large pot, heat the oil over medium heat.
    • Add the diced onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add the diced carrots and potatoes to the pot and cook for another 5 minutes.
  3. Add the meat to the pot and cook until browned on all sides, about 10 minutes.
  4. Stir in the curry roux and add enough water to the pot to make a thick sauce, about 2-3 cups.
  5. Add the garam masala, turmeric, cumin, coriander, garlic powder, and salt to the pot. Stir to combine.
  6. Bring the sauce to a simmer, reduce the heat to low, and let cook for 20-30 minutes, until the vegetables are tender and the flavors have melded together.
  7. Serve the Japanese curry over steamed rice.

Note: You can adjust the spices to your liking, or add other seasonings such as ginger or cardamom to the sauce. You can also add other vegetables, such as bell peppers or mushrooms, to the sauce as desired.

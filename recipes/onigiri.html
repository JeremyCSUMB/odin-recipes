Onigiri

Onigiri is a popular Japanese snack food made of rice that is molded into a triangular or cylindrical shape and often filled with various ingredients such as salmon, tuna, umeboshi (pickled plum), or katsuobushi (dried bonito flakes). Onigiri is wrapped in nori (dried seaweed) to hold the filling inside and is a portable and convenient food that can be enjoyed as a snack or a meal. It is a staple food in Japan and is widely available in convenience stores, supermarkets, and food vendors. Onigiri is often made using leftover rice from the previous meal and is a simple and affordable way to enjoy a tasty and filling meal on the go.

Ingredients:

  1. 2 cups sushi rice
  2. 2 tablespoons rice vinegar
  3. 1 teaspoon salt
  4. 1 teaspoon sugar
  5. Nori seaweed
  6. Optional fillings of your choice (e.g. salmon, tuna, umeboshi, katsuobushi, etc.)

Instructions:

  1. Cook the sushi rice according to package instructions.
  2. In a small bowl, mix together the rice vinegar, salt, and sugar until the salt and sugar have dissolved.
  3. Pour the vinegar mixture over the cooked rice and stir to combine.
  4. Let the rice cool to room temperature.
  5. Cut the nori seaweed into rectangles.
  6. Take a small handful of the rice and shape it into a triangle or cylinder.
  7. Place a piece of nori on a flat surface, with the shiny side facing down.
  8. Place the shaped rice on top of the nori.
  9. If using a filling, place it on top of the rice.
  10. Fold the nori over the rice and filling, sealing the edges.
  11. Repeat the process with the remaining rice and nori.

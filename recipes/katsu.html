Tonkatsu

Tonkatsu is a popular dish in Japanese cuisine that consists of a breaded, deep-fried pork cutlet. The pork is usually pounded thin, coated in breadcrumbs, and deep-fried until crispy on the outside and juicy on the inside. It is commonly served with a tonkatsu sauce, a sweet and savory sauce that is similar to Worcestershire sauce, and served over rice or with shredded cabbage. Tonkatsu is a staple of Japanese comfort food and can be found at many restaurants in Japan.

Ingredients:

  • 4 pork cutlets, about 1-inch thick
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 1/2 cups breadcrumbs
  • Salt and pepper
  • Vegetable oil, for frying

Instructions:

  1. Place a piece of plastic wrap over the pork cutlets and pound them with a meat mallet until they are 1/4-inch thick.
  2. Season the pork cutlets with salt and pepper.
  3. Put the flour in a shallow dish. Beat the eggs in another shallow dish. Put the breadcrumbs in a third shallow dish.
  4. Dredge each pork cutlet in the flour, shaking off any excess. Dip it in the beaten eggs, letting any excess egg drain off. Finally, coat it well with breadcrumbs.
  5. Heat about 1/2 inch of vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat until hot.
  6. Fry the pork cutlets until golden brown and crispy, about 3-4 minutes per side.
  7. Remove the pork cutlets from the skillet and drain on paper towels.
  8. Serve the tonkatsu with rice and tonkatsu sauce on the side. Enjoy!
